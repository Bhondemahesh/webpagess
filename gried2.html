The grapevine is a stem- tendril climbing plant that grows upwards with vigorous overhanging (cascading) growth-- new shoots may grow 1 - 4 metres every year. It is usually cultivated as a shaped shrub with a growth height of 10 metres or more, depending on the pruning technique.

This variety was developed by Mr. T. R. Dabade of Nannaj, Solapur selecting the buds on cardons of Thompson Seedless variety in 1982.

Characteristics
  • High fruitfulness & simultaneous ripening of the bunches
  • The bunches weigh around 400 to 500 gm
  • Berries are 1 to 1.5 inches long
  • During ripening the berries turn yellowish
